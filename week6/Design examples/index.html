Design Principles Examples

  1. Affordance

    ++Engadget: The navigation header has the home page button in the top left and the rest of the blog topics across the top.
    --Project Free TV: The navigation from the home page is in the middle of the screen.

  2. Mapping

    ++Paypal: The submit button is underneath the sign-up form.
    --Amazon: The proceed to checkout buttons are on the right in the shopping cart page instead of being on the bottom of the page like most "proceed" buttons.

  3. Chunking

    ++Facebook: The news feed breaks the status updates and posts into seperate sections.
    --Reddit: The posts are bunched together with no seperation.

  4. Signal-to-Noise Ratio

    ++Twitter: The main content and tweets are isolated and emphasized in the middle of the page.
    --Linkedin: There are links to different recommended people, places, and information all along the right side of the page.

  5. Hick's Law

    ++Groupon: The starting home page for Groupon is just a box to enter your email with no unnecessary buttons or links.
    --Espn: The Espn header has a ton of buttons and links to different information.
